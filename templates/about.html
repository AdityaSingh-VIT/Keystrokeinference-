What are Side-Channel Attacks?

Side-channel attacks extract information from the physical implementation of a system rather than finding weaknesses in the algorithms themselves. These attacks analyze information gained from the physical operation of systems, such as:

Acoustic Emissions

Sound produced by keyboards, printers, or computer components during operation.

Power Consumption

Variations in energy use that can reveal operations being performed.

Timing Information

Differences in how long operations take to complete.

Keyboard Acoustic Attacks

This application demonstrates a specific type of side-channel attack that exploits the sounds produced when typing on a keyboard. Each key on a keyboard produces a slightly different sound when pressed, due to:

  • Physical position on the keyboard
  • Differences in key mechanism and construction
  • Variations in typing force and style
  • Resonance patterns of the keyboard and surrounding materials

By recording these sounds and analyzing them with machine learning techniques, it's possible to reconstruct what was typed, even without visual access to the keyboard.
